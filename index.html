Chasen Jeffries is a PhD student studying International Political Economy and Computation Analytics at Claremont Graduate University. His work uses a variety of quantitative and qualitative methodologies to investigate a range of political economy topics including Intellectual Property Protections, China's Political Economy, Human Capital Accumulation, and International Political Economy.

Chasen has published in Journal of Policy Analysis and Management and Columbia Journal of History.
He has presented at conferences including: International Congress on Information and Communication Technology (ICICT), American Historical Association (AHA), and State of North Carolina Undergraduate Research and Creativity Symposium (SNCURCS).



About

Chasen Jeffries grew up in the Appalachian mountains on the border of Tennessee and North Carolina where he enjoyed the outdoors and aspired to be a professional soccer player. In 2019, he received a B.A. in History from High Point University where he studied history, with an emphasis on modern European and Asian history. Shortly after graduating, he accepted a position at Red 5 Security as a researcher and analyst where he conducted fast-paced open source research to achieve client objectives. In fall 2021, he begin pursuing a graduate degree at Claremont Graduate University.
