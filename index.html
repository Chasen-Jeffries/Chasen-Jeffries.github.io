Chasen Jeffries is a driven PhD candidate at Claremont Graduate University, where his work in International Political Economy and Computational Analytics is breaking new ground. With a keen focus on blending qualitative and quantitative research methods, Chasen adeptly merges traditional data science with advanced computational techniques to explore and unravel the complexities of global economic and political systems.

Chasen's work is characterized by its rigorous approach and innovative methodology. He is actively engaged in groundbreaking research on the political economy of patent systems and China's Political Economy. His scholarly contributions, including papers in esteemed journals such as the Journal of Policy Analysis and Management and presentations at international conferences, underscore his deep understanding of economic and political theory.

Chasen's portfolio is a testament to his versatile analytical, coding, and data skills. His projects include predictive modeling of political instability, NLP analysis of US Congress tweets, and Simulations of Olson's Bandits. He demonstrates proficiency in a variety of tools, including ChatGPT, and programming languages, which he applies in developing applications like the Prepare R package.

As he continues to bridge the gap between theory and practice, Chasen Jeffries is emerging as a prominent figure in the intersection of political economy and data science. His work not only contributes significant academic insights but also offers practical, data-driven solutions for industry challenges. Poised to make substantial contributions to both the academic world and beyond, Chasen's unique blend of skills positions him as a dynamic and influential voice in his field.



About

Chasen Jeffries was raised in the beautiful Appalachian mountains, nestled on the border of Tennessee and North Carolina, where he developed a deep appreciation for the outdoors and nurtured his dream of becoming a professional soccer player. In 2019, Chasen earned his Bachelor of Arts degree in History from High Point University. During his undergraduate studies, he explored a variety of historical topics, with a particular emphasis on modern European and Asian history. Shortly after completing his undergraduate degree, Chasen joined Red 5 Security as a researcher and analyst. In this role, he conducted dynamic open source research, working diligently to achieve client objectives in a fast-paced environment. In the fall of 2021, Chasen embarked on his journey to pursue a graduate degree at Claremont Graduate University, where he is currently immersing himself in the realms of International Political Economy and Computation Analytics.
